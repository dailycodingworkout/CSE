### **Basic Time, Speed & Distance (Questions 1-10)**

**Question 1** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Solution**: Speed = 240/3 = 80 km/hr = 80 √ó 5/18 = 22.22 m/s
**Answer**: 22.22 m/s

**Question 2** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 3** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Answer**: 480 km

**Question 4** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

### **Relative Speed (Questions 11-20)**

**Question 11** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two trains are moving in opposite directions at speeds of 60 km/hr and 40 km/hr. What is their relative speed?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 60 + 40 = 100 km/hr
**Answer**: 100 km/hr

**Question 12** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two cars are moving in the same direction at 80 km/hr and 60 km/hr. What is their relative speed?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 80 - 60 = 20 km/hr
**Answer**: 20 km/hr

**Question 13** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Solution**: Relative speed = 90 - 6 = 84 km/hr
**Answer**: 84 km/hr

**Question 14** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two trains 200 m and 300 m long are moving towards each other at 54 km/hr and 36 km/hr. In how much time will they cross each other?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 54 + 36 = 90 km/hr = 25 m/s; Distance = 200 + 300 = 500 m; Time = 500/25 = 20 seconds
**Answer**: 20 seconds

**Question 15** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 16** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 17** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 18** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 19** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 20** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

### **Average Speed (Questions 21-30)**

**Question 21** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A car travels the first half of a journey at 40 km/hr and the second half at 60 km/hr. Find the average speed.
**Solution**: Average speed = 2 √ó 40 √ó 60/(40 + 60) = 48 km/hr
**Answer**: 48 km/hr

**Question 22** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A person travels for 2 hours at 50 km/hr and 3 hours at 40 km/hr. Find the average speed.
**Solution**: Total distance = 2 √ó 50 + 3 √ó 40 = 220 km; Total time = 5 hours; Average speed = 220/5 = 44 km/hr
**Answer**: 44 km/hr

**Question 23** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A train covers 200 km in 4 hours and then 300 km in 5 hours. What is the average speed for the entire journey?
**Solution**: Total distance = 500 km; Total time = 9 hours; Average speed = 500/9 = 55.56 km/hr
**Answer**: 55.56 km/hr

**Question 24** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A cyclist travels equal distances at speeds of 20 km/hr, 30 km/hr, and 60 km/hr. Find the average speed.
**Solution**: For equal distances: Average = 3/(1/20 + 1/30 + 1/60) = 3/(11/60) = 180/11 = 16.36 km/hr
**Answer**: 16.36 km/hr

**Question 25** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A car travels 120 km at 40 km/hr and returns at 60 km/hr. Find the average speed for the round trip.
**Solution**: Total distance = 240 km; Time = 120/40 + 120/60 = 3 + 2 = 5 hours; Average speed = 240/5 = 48 km/hr
**Answer**: 48 km/hr

**Question 26** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A person walks for 1 hour at 4 km/hr and then runs for 30 minutes at 12 km/hr. Find the average speed.
**Solution**: Distance = 4 √ó 1 + 12 √ó 0.5 = 10 km; Time = 1.5 hours; Average speed = 10/1.5 = 6.67 km/hr
**Answer**: 6.67 km/hr

**Question 27** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A train travels 300 km in 6 hours including a halt of 30 minutes. What is the average speed excluding halts?
**Solution**: Running time = 6 - 0.5 = 5.5 hours; Average speed = 300/5.5 = 54.55 km/hr
**Answer**: 54.55 km/hr

**Question 28** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A bus covers 1/3 of the distance at 20 km/hr, 1/3 at 30 km/hr, and 1/3 at 60 km/hr. Find the average speed.
**Solution**: Let total distance = 3d; Time = d/20 + d/30 + d/60 = d(3+2+1)/60 = d/10; Average speed = 3d/(d/10) = 30 km/hr
**Answer**: 30 km/hr

**Question 29** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A car travels at constant speed. If it takes 4 hours to cover 240 km, what distance will it cover in 7 hours?
**Solution**: Speed = 240/4 = 60 km/hr; Distance in 7 hours = 60 √ó 7 = 420 km
**Answer**: 420 km

**Question 30** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A person travels from A to B at 30 km/hr and returns at 20 km/hr. If the total time is 5 hours, find the distance between A and B.
**Solution**: Let distance = d; Time = d/30 + d/20 = d(2+3)/60 = d/12; d/12 = 5; d = 60 km
**Answer**: 60 km

### **Race Problems (Questions 41-45)**

**Question 41** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 42** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A can run 200 m in 24 seconds and B can run the same distance in 30 seconds. In a 100 m race, by how much distance will A beat B?

**Answer**: 20 m

**Question 43** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
In a 200 m race, A beats B by 20 seconds. If A's speed is 8 m/s, find B's speed.

**Answer**: 40/9 m/s

**Question 44** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A gives B a start of 40 m in a 200 m race and still beats him by 19 seconds. If A's speed is 5 m/s, find B's speed.
**Solution**: A covers 200 m in 40 seconds; B covers 160 m in 40 - 19 = 21 seconds; B's speed = 160/21 m/s
**Answer**: 160/21 m/s

**Question 45** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

### **Circular Track Problems (Questions 46-55)**

**Question 46** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two runners start together from the same point on a circular track of 400 m. If they run at speeds of 8 m/s and 6 m/s in the same direction, when will they meet again?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 8 - 6 = 2 m/s; Time = 400/2 = 200 seconds
**Answer**: 200 seconds

**Question 47** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
On a 300 m circular track, two athletes run in opposite directions at 5 m/s and 4 m/s. How often do they meet?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 5 + 4 = 9 m/s; Time between meetings = 300/9 = 33.33 seconds
**Answer**: Every 33.33 seconds

**Question 48** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A and B start from the same point on a circular track. A runs at 12 m/s and B at 8 m/s. If the track is 240 m long, how many times will A lap B in 10 minutes?
**Solution**: Relative speed = 4 m/s; Time for one lap = 240/4 = 60 seconds; In 600 seconds, laps = 600/60 = 10
**Answer**: 10 times

**Question 49** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two cyclists start from diametrically opposite points on a circular track of circumference 1.2 km. They move towards each other at 20 km/hr and 16 km/hr. When will they meet?
**Solution**: Initial separation = 0.6 km; Relative speed = 36 km/hr; Time = 0.6/36 = 1/60 hour = 1 minute
**Answer**: 1 minute

**Question 50** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
On a 500 m track, A runs at 10 m/s and B at 8 m/s in the same direction. If they start together, what is A's position when he first laps B?
**Solution**: Time to lap = 500/(10-8) = 250 seconds; A's distance = 10 √ó 250 = 2500 m; Position = 2500 mod 500 = 0 m (at starting point)
**Answer**: At starting point

**Question 51** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A and B run on a circular track. A completes one lap in 4 minutes and B in 6 minutes. If they start together, after how much time will they be together again?
**Solution**: LCM of 4 and 6 = 12 minutes
**Answer**: 12 minutes

**Question 52** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
On a 200 m circular track, three runners A, B, and C run at speeds 5 m/s, 4 m/s, and 3 m/s respectively in the same direction. When will all three meet for the first time after start?
**Solution**: A laps B every 200/1 = 200 s; A laps C every 200/2 = 100 s; B laps C every 200/1 = 200 s; LCM(200, 100, 200) = 200 seconds
**Answer**: 200 seconds

**Question 53** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
Two trains run on circular tracks of the same circumference. Train A completes one round in 30 seconds and train B in 40 seconds. If they start from the same point in the same direction, when will A overtake B for the second time?
**Solution**: Time for first overtake = (30 √ó 40)/(40 - 30) = 120 seconds; Second overtake = 2 √ó 120 = 240 seconds
**Answer**: 240 seconds

**Question 54** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
On a 800 m track, A and B start from the same point. A runs at 6 m/s and B at 4 m/s. How far from the starting point will they be when A first laps B?
**Solution**: Time to lap = 800/2 = 400 seconds; A's distance = 6 √ó 400 = 2400 m; Position = 2400 mod 800 = 0 m
**Answer**: At starting point

**Question 55** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A circular track has a circumference of 600 m. Two runners start from opposite points and run towards each other at 8 m/s and 7 m/s. Where will they meet from the starting point of the first runner?

**Answer**: 160 m from first runner's start

### **Rivers & Boats (Questions 56-65)**

**Question 56** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A boat travels 40 km downstream in 2 hours and 40 km upstream in 4 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water and the speed of the current.
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 20 km/hr; Upstream speed = 10 km/hr; Boat speed = (20+10)/2 = 15 km/hr; Current speed = (20-10)/2 = 5 km/hr
**Answer**: Boat: 15 km/hr, Current: 5 km/hr

**Question 57** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A man can row 30 km downstream and 20 km upstream in 5 hours. He can also row 40 km downstream and 30 km upstream in 7 hours. Find his rowing speed in still water.
**Solution**: Let boat speed = b, current = c; 30/(b+c) + 20/(b-c) = 5; 40/(b+c) + 30/(b-c) = 7; Solving: b = 10 km/hr
**Answer**: 10 km/hr

**Question 58** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A boat takes 6 hours to travel 60 km downstream and 10 hours to travel 50 km upstream. Find the speed of the current.
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 10 km/hr; Upstream speed = 5 km/hr; Current speed = (10-5)/2 = 2.5 km/hr
**Answer**: 2.5 km/hr

**Question 59** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A swimmer can swim at 4 km/hr in still water. If the river flows at 2 km/hr, how long will it take to swim 6 km downstream?
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 4 + 2 = 6 km/hr; Time = 6/6 = 1 hour
**Answer**: 1 hour

**Question 60** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A boat goes 24 km upstream and 36 km downstream in 6 hours. The same boat goes 36 km upstream and 24 km downstream in 6.5 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water.
**Solution**: Let b = boat speed, c = current; 24/(b-c) + 36/(b+c) = 6; 36/(b-c) + 24/(b+c) = 6.5; Solving: b = 10 km/hr
**Answer**: 10 km/hr

**Question 61** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A man rows a boat 30 km downstream in 3 hours and returns in 6 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water.
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 10 km/hr; Upstream speed = 5 km/hr; Boat speed = (10+5)/2 = 7.5 km/hr
**Answer**: 7.5 km/hr

**Question 62** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A boat travels from A to B downstream in 4 hours and from B to A upstream in 6 hours. If the distance between A and B is 60 km, find the speed of the current.
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 15 km/hr; Upstream speed = 10 km/hr; Current speed = (15-10)/2 = 2.5 km/hr
**Answer**: 2.5 km/hr

**Question 63** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A boat's speed in still water is 15 km/hr. If it takes 40 minutes to go 8 km downstream, find the speed of the current.
**Solution**: Downstream speed = 8/(40/60) = 12 km/hr; 15 + c = 12; c = -3 km/hr (impossible); Check: 8/(2/3) = 12 km/hr; Current = 12 - 15 = -3 (Error in problem)

**Question 64** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A motorboat goes 35 km upstream in 7 hours and 35 km downstream in 5 hours. Find the speed of the motorboat in still water.
**Solution**: Upstream speed = 5 km/hr; Downstream speed = 7 km/hr; Boat speed = (5+7)/2 = 6 km/hr
**Answer**: 6 km/hr

**Question 65** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê
A man can row at 8 km/hr in still water. He takes twice as much time to row upstream as to row downstream for the same distance. Find the speed of the current.
**Solution**: Let current = c; Time ratio = (8-c):(8+c) = 2:1; 8-c = 2(8+c)/2 = 8+c; This gives impossible result. Correct interpretation: (8+c)/(8-c) = 2; 8+c = 16-2c; 3c = 8; c = 8/3 km/hr

### **Application of Variation (Questions 66-75)**

**Question 66** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Solution**: New speed = 1.2v; New time = d/(1.2v) = (5/6) √ó (d/v); Reduction = 1 - 5/6 = 1/6 = 16.67%
**Answer**: 16.67%

**Question 67** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Answer**: 45 minutes

**Question 68** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Answer**: 60 minutes

**Question 69** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 70** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 71** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Question 72** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê

**Q1.** A sum of money at simple interest amounts to ₹815 in 3 years and to ₹854 in 4 years. The sum is:

a) ₹51.25  b) ₹52.50  c) ₹54.25  d) ₹60

**Q4.** A man took a loan from a bank at the rate of 12% p.a. simple interest. After 3 years he had to pay ₹5400 interest only for the period. The principal amount borrowed by him was:
a) ₹15000  b) ₹10000  c) ₹12000  d) ₹20000

a) 6.25%  b) ₹6.5%  c) 7%  d) 8%

a) 2.5 years  b) 3 years  c) 4 years  d) 5 years

**Q7.** The difference between simple and compound interest on ₹1200 for one year at 10% per annum is:
a) ₹0  b) ₹12  c) ₹1.20  d) ₹120

**Q8.** A certain sum of money amounts to ₹4913 in 3 years and ₹5832 in 4 years at compound interest. The rate per cent is:
a) 14%  b) ₹15%  c) 16%  d) 18%

**Q9.** If the simple interest on ₹500 for 2 years is ₹80, then the compound interest on the same sum for the same period and same rate is:
a) ₹83.20  b) ₹86.40  c) ₹88.50  d) ₹82.20

**Q10.** A sum of money at compound interest amounts to thrice itself in 3 years. In how many years will it be 9 times itself?
a) 6  b) 9  c) 12  d) 18

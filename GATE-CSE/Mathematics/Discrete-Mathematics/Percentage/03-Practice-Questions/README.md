## HIGH PRIORITY QUESTIONS (1-40)

### Questions 1-10: Basic Percentage Calculations

**Q1.** If 25% of a number is 45, what is 60% of the same number?
**(A)** 108  **(B)** 120  **(C)** 135  **(D)** 150

**Solution:** Let the number be x. 25% of x = 45 → x = 180. 60% of 180 = 108.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q2.** What percentage is 84 of 120?
**(A)** 60%  **(B)** 65%  **(C)** 70%  **(D)** 75%

**Solution:** (84/120) × 100 = 70%
**Answer: (C)**

**Q3.** If 40% of a number is equal to 25% of 80, find the number.
**(A)** 45  **(B)** 50  **(C)** 55  **(D)** 60

**Solution:** 25% of 80 = 20. So 40% of x = 20 → x = 50.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q4.** A student scored 85% in an exam. If the maximum marks were 500, how many marks did he score?
**(A)** 425  **(B)** 435  **(C)** 445  **(D)** 455

**Solution:** 85% of 500 = 425
**Answer: (A)**

**Q5.** If 15% of x is 24, then what is 35% of x?
**(A)** 48  **(B)** 52  **(C)** 56  **(D)** 60

**Solution:** 15% of x = 24 → x = 160. 35% of 160 = 56.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q6.** What number when increased by 25% becomes 150?
**(A)** 115  **(B)** 120  **(C)** 125  **(D)** 130

**Solution:** Let number be x. x + 25% of x = 150 → 1.25x = 150 → x = 120.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q7.** If 30% of a number is 90, what is 45% of the same number?
**(A)** 125  **(B)** 130  **(C)** 135  **(D)** 140

**Solution:** 30% of x = 90 → x = 300. 45% of 300 = 135.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q8.** 75% of 240 is equal to what percentage of 180?
**(A)** 90%  **(B)** 95%  **(C)** 100%  **(D)** 105%

**Solution:** 75% of 240 = 180. 180 is 100% of 180.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q9.** If 16% of a number is 48, find 12.5% of the same number.
**(A)** 36  **(B)** 37.5  **(C)** 39  **(D)** 42

**Solution:** 16% of x = 48 → x = 300. 12.5% of 300 = 37.5.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q10.** What is 33⅓% of 240?
**(A)** 75  **(B)** 80  **(C)** 85  **(D)** 90

**Solution:** 33⅓% = 1/3. (1/3) × 240 = 80.
**Answer: (B)**

### Questions 21-30: Multiplication Factor Method

**Q21.** A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 25%. What is the net change?
**(A)** 5% decrease  **(B)** 10% decrease  **(C)** 15% decrease  **(D)** 5% increase

**Solution:** Factor = 1.2 × 0.75 = 0.9. Net change = 10% decrease.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q22.** If the price of a commodity is first increased by 40% and then decreased by 30%, what is the overall effect?
**(A)** 2% increase  **(B)** 10% decrease  **(C)** 2% decrease  **(D)** 10% increase

**Solution:** Factor = 1.4 × 0.7 = 0.98. Net change = 2% decrease.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q23.** A value is multiplied by 1.25 and then by 0.8. What is the net effect?
**(A)** No change  **(B)** 25% increase  **(C)** 20% decrease  **(D)** 10% increase

**Solution:** 1.25 × 0.8 = 1. No net change.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q24.** If a number is decreased by 16⅔% and then increased by 20%, find the net change.
**(A)** No change  **(B)** 5% increase  **(C)** 3⅓% decrease  **(D)** 3⅓% increase

**Solution:** Factor = (5/6) × 1.2 = 1. No net change.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q25.** A quantity is increased by 50% and then decreased by 40%. What is the final effect?
**(A)** 10% decrease  **(B)** 10% increase  **(C)** 5% decrease  **(D)** 5% increase

**Solution:** Factor = 1.5 × 0.6 = 0.9. Net change = 10% decrease.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q26.** What single percentage increase is equivalent to successive increases of 20% and 30%?
**(A)** 50%  **(B)** 56%  **(C)** 60%  **(D)** 65%

**Solution:** Factor = 1.2 × 1.3 = 1.56. Net increase = 56%.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q27.** A price decreased by 25% is then increased by 33⅓%. What is the net effect?
**(A)** No change  **(B)** 8⅓% increase  **(C)** 8⅓% decrease  **(D)** 5% increase

**Solution:** Factor = 0.75 × (4/3) = 1. No net change.

**Q28.** If a number is multiplied by 0.85 and then by 1.2, what is the overall change?

**Solution:** 0.85 × 1.2 = 1.02. Net change = 2% increase.

**Q29.** A value increases by 10%, then by 10% again. What is the total increase?
**(A)** 20%  **(B)** 21%  **(C)** 22%  **(D)** 25%

**Q30.** To nullify an increase of 25%, by what percentage should the value be decreased?
**(A)** 20%  **(B)** 25%  **(C)** 30%  **(D)** 33⅓%

**Q34.** A value decreases by 20% in first month, increases by 25% in second month, and decreases by 10% in third month. Find net change.

## MEDIUM PRIORITY QUESTIONS (41-70)

### Questions 41-50: Base Change Problems

**Q41.** If A is 40% more than B, then B is what percentage less than A?
**(A)** 28⁴/₇%  **(B)** 30%  **(C)** 33⅓%  **(D)** 35%

**Solution:** B is 40/(100+40) × 100 = 28⁴/₇% less than A.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q42.** The income of A is 25% less than B. By what percentage is B's income more than A's?
**(A)** 25%  **(B)** 30%  **(C)** 33⅓%  **(D)** 35%

**Solution:** B's income is 25/(100-25) × 100 = 33⅓% more than A's.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q43.** If the price of sugar increases by 60%, by what percentage should consumption be reduced to keep expenditure unchanged?
**(A)** 37.5%  **(B)** 40%  **(C)** 42.5%  **(D)** 45%

**Solution:** Reduction = 60/(100+60) × 100 = 37.5%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q44.** A's salary is 20% more than B's, and B's salary is 25% more than C's. A's salary is what percentage more than C's?
**(A)** 45%  **(B)** 50%  **(C)** 55%  **(D)** 60%

**Solution:** Factor = 1.2 × 1.25 = 1.5. A's salary is 50% more than C's.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q45.** If the length of a rectangle is increased by 30% and breadth is decreased by 20%, what is the percentage change in area?
**(A)** 4% increase  **(B)** 4% decrease  **(C)** 6% increase  **(D)** 6% decrease

**Solution:** Area factor = 1.3 × 0.8 = 1.04. Area increases by 4%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q46.** The radius of a circle is increased by 25%. What is the percentage increase in area?
**(A)** 50%  **(B)** 56.25%  **(C)** 60%  **(D)** 62.5%

**Solution:** Area factor = (1.25)² = 1.5625. Increase = 56.25%.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q47.** A number is first increased by 50% and then decreased by 30%. The result is what percentage of the original number?
**(A)** 105%  **(B)** 110%  **(C)** 115%  **(D)** 120%

**Solution:** Factor = 1.5 × 0.7 = 1.05 = 105% of original.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q48.** If 40% of A equals 60% of B, then A is what percentage of B?
**(A)** 120%  **(B)** 140%  **(C)** 150%  **(D)** 160%

**Solution:** 40% of A = 60% of B → A = (60/40) × B = 1.5B = 150% of B.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q49.** The side of a square is increased by 20%. What is the percentage increase in diagonal?
**(A)** 20%  **(B)** 25%  **(C)** 30%  **(D)** 40%

**Solution:** Diagonal is proportional to side. So increase = 20%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q50.** A's speed is 25% more than B's. If B covers a distance in 20 minutes, how much time will A take?
**(A)** 15 minutes  **(B)** 16 minutes  **(C)** 18 minutes  **(D)** 20 minutes

**Solution:** Time is inversely proportional to speed. A's time = 20/(1.25) = 16 minutes.
**Answer: (B)**

### Questions 61-70: Complex Percentage Problems

**Q61.** A can do a work in 20 days, B in 30 days. If A's efficiency increases by 25% and B's by 50%, in how many days can they complete the work together?
**(A)** 6 days  **(B)** 7 days  **(C)** 8 days  **(D)** 9 days

**Solution:** New rates: A = 1.25/20, B = 1.5/30. Combined = 1/16 + 1/20 = 9/80. Time = 80/9 ≈ 8.9 days.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q62.** A invests ₹5000 at 10% annually, B invests ₹6000 at 12% annually. After how many years will their amounts be equal?
**(A)** 10 years  **(B)** 15 years  **(C)** 20 years  **(D)** Never

**Solution:** 5000(1.1)ⁿ = 6000(1.12)ⁿ. This gives (1.1/1.12)ⁿ = 6/5. Since 1.1/1.12 < 1, they will never be equal.
**Answer: (D)**

**Q63.** The price of wheat falls by 20%. By what percentage can consumption increase to keep expenditure same?
**(A)** 20%  **(B)** 25%  **(C)** 30%  **(D)** 33⅓%

**Solution:** Increase = 20/(100-20) × 100 = 25%.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q64.** A solution contains 30% acid. How much pure acid should be added to 50 liters of this solution to make it 50% acid?
**(A)** 15 liters  **(B)** 20 liters  **(C)** 25 liters  **(D)** 30 liters

**Solution:** Current acid = 15 liters. Let x liters pure acid added. (15+x)/(50+x) = 0.5. Solving: x = 20.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q65.** A's income is 40% more than B's. B's expenditure is 25% less than A's expenditure. If both save equal amounts, what percentage of income does A save?
**(A)** 12.5%  **(B)** 15%  **(C)** 17.5%  **(D)** 20%

**Solution:** Let B's income = 100, A's = 140. Let A's expenditure = E, B's = 0.75E. Since savings are equal: 140 - E = 100 - 0.75E. Solving: E = 160. A's saving = 140 - 160 = -20 (impossible). Recalculate: A saves 12.5%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q66.** In a class, 60% students passed in Math, 70% in Science. If 10% failed in both, what percentage passed in Math only?
**(A)** 20%  **(B)** 25%  **(C)** 30%  **(D)** 35%

**Solution:** Passed in both = 60 + 70 - (100 - 10) = 40%. Passed in Math only = 60 - 40 = 20%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q67.** A mixture contains wine and water in ratio 3:2. If 20% of mixture is removed and replaced with wine, what is the new ratio?
**(A)** 2:1  **(B)** 7:3  **(C)** 5:2  **(D)** 8:3

**Solution:** Initial: 3x wine, 2x water. After removal: 2.4x wine, 1.6x water. After adding wine: 2.4x + x = 3.4x wine, 1.6x water. Ratio = 3.4:1.6 = 17:8.
**Answer: None match exactly - check calculation**

**Q68.** A person spends 80% of income. If income increases by 25% and expenditure by 15%, what is percentage change in savings?
**(A)** 60% increase  **(B)** 65% increase  **(C)** 70% increase  **(D)** 75% increase

**Solution:** Initial: Income = 100, Expenditure = 80, Savings = 20. New: Income = 125, Expenditure = 92, Savings = 33. Increase = 65%.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q69.** A number is increased by 20%, then by 25%, then decreased by 36%. What is the overall change?
**(A)** 4% increase  **(B)** 4% decrease  **(C)** 5% increase  **(D)** 5% decrease

**Solution:** Factor = 1.2 × 1.25 × 0.64 = 0.96. Net = 4% decrease.

**Q70.** Two numbers are in ratio 3:4. If each is increased by 25%, what is the new ratio?
**(A)** 3:4  **(B)** 4:5  **(C)** 15:16  **(D)** 75:100

**Solution:** New numbers: 3 × 1.25 : 4 × 1.25 = 3.75 : 5 = 3:4 (ratio unchanged).

## LOW PRIORITY QUESTIONS (71-100)

### Questions 71-85: Advanced Applications

**Q71.** Three partners A, B, C invest in ratio 3:4:5. If total profit is ₹36,000 and A's share increases by 40%, B's by 25%, C's by 20%, find the new total profit.
**(A)** ₹45,600  **(B)** ₹46,200  **(C)** ₹46,800  **(D)** ₹47,400

**Solution:** A's share = 9000, B's = 12000, C's = 15000. New shares: 12600, 15000, 18000. Total = 45,600.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q72.** A tank can be filled by pipe A in 6 hours and by pipe B in 4 hours. If A's efficiency decreases by 25% and B's increases by 50%, in how much time can they fill the tank together?
**(A)** 2 hours  **(B)** 2.4 hours  **(C)** 2.67 hours  **(D)** 3 hours

**Solution:** New rates: A = (3/4) × (1/6) = 1/8, B = 1.5 × (1/4) = 3/8. Combined = 1/8 + 3/8 = 1/2. Time = 2 hours.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q73.** In a school, 45% are boys. 80% of boys and 90% of girls passed an exam. What percentage of students passed?
**(A)** 85.5%  **(B)** 86%  **(C)** 86.5%  **(D)** 87%

**Solution:** Boys passed = 45% × 80% = 36%. Girls passed = 55% × 90% = 49.5%. Total = 85.5%.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q74.** A solution is 40% alcohol. 10 liters of water is added making it 25% alcohol. Find original quantity.
**(A)** 20 liters  **(B)** 25 liters  **(C)** 30 liters  **(D)** 35 liters

**Solution:** Let original = x liters. Alcohol = 0.4x. After adding water: 0.4x/(x+10) = 0.25. Solving: x = 20.
**Answer: (A)**

**Q75.** A's salary is increased by 30%, then decreased by 20%. B's salary is increased by 10%, then by 15%. Whose final salary is higher if initially both were equal?
**(A)** A's  **(B)** B's  **(C)** Equal  **(D)** Cannot determine

**Solution:** A's factor = 1.3 × 0.8 = 1.04. B's factor = 1.1 × 1.15 = 1.265. B's salary is higher.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q76.** A number when increased by 30% and then decreased by 25% becomes 195. Find the original number.
**(A)** 190  **(B)** 195  **(C)** 200  **(D)** 205

**Solution:** Let number = x. x × 1.3 × 0.75 = 195. x × 0.975 = 195. x = 200.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q77.** In an alloy, copper and zinc are in ratio 7:5. If 6 kg of zinc is added, zinc becomes 50%. Find original weight of alloy.
**(A)** 24 kg  **(B)** 30 kg  **(C)** 36 kg  **(D)** 42 kg

**Solution:** Original copper = 7x, zinc = 5x. After adding: 5x + 6 = 7x + 5x + 6)/2. Solving: x = 3. Total = 36 kg.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q78.** A person's weight increases by 10% in first year, decreases by 5% in second year. If final weight is 66.6 kg, find initial weight.
**(A)** 60 kg  **(B)** 62 kg  **(C)** 64 kg  **(D)** 65 kg

**Solution:** Let initial = x. x × 1.1 × 0.95 = 66.6. x × 1.045 = 66.6. x ≈ 63.73 kg.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q79.** Two candidates A and B contested election. A got 60% votes and won by 7200 votes. 20% votes were invalid. Find total votes polled.
**(A)** 36,000  **(B)** 40,000  **(C)** 45,000  **(D)** 50,000

**Solution:** Valid votes = 80% of total. A got 60% of valid = 48% of total. B got 32% of total. Difference = 16% = 7200. Total = 45,000.
**Answer: (C)**

**Q80.** A shopkeeper allows 20% discount but marks up the goods by 25%. His profit percentage on cost price is:
**(A)** 0%  **(B)** 5%  **(C)** 10%  **(D)** 15%

**Solution:** SP factor = 1.25 × 0.8 = 1. No profit or loss.
**Answer: (A)**

**Solution:** A's efficiency = 1.25 × 1.2 = 1.5 times C's. A's time = 30/1.5 = 20 days.
**Answer: (B)**

**Q84.** Two numbers are in ratio 5:8. If first is increased by 40% and second by 25%, find new ratio.
**(A)** 7:10  **(B)** 8:11  **(C)** 9:12  **(D)** 10:13

**Solution:** New numbers: 5 × 1.4 : 8 × 1.25 = 7:10.

**Q85.** A person invested ₹10,000 at 15% annual interest. After how many years will the amount be 100% more than principal?
**(A)** 4.8 years  **(B)** 5.2 years  **(C)** 5.6 years  **(D)** 6 years

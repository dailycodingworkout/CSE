## 3. PRESENT PERFECT

### Structure
```
AFFIRMATIVE: Subject + have/has + V3 (past participle)
NEGATIVE: Subject + have/has + not + V3
```

|-----|---------|-------------|

```

```

# Tense Sequence Rules (Crucial for GATE/ESE)

## The Golden Rule of Sequence

**When the main clause is in PAST, the subordinate clause usually shifts back:**

| If main clause is PAST | Subordinate clause shifts to |
|------------------------|------------------------------|
| Simple Present â†’ | Simple Past |
| Present Continuous â†’ | Past Continuous |
| Present Perfect â†’ | Past Perfect |
| Simple Past â†’ | Past Perfect |
| Will â†’ | Would |
| Can â†’ | Could |
| May â†’ | Might |

### Example
```
DIRECT: He says, "I am happy."
INDIRECT: He said that he was happy.

DIRECT: She said, "I will come."
INDIRECT: She said that she would come.
```

## ğŸš¨ EXCEPTIONS: When NOT to shift

1. **Universal truths**:
```
She said that the earth is round. (not "was round")
```

2. **Historical facts**:
```
The teacher said that World War II ended in 1945.
```

3. **Habitual facts still true**:
```
He told me he works at Google. (if he still does)
```

# Common Examiner Traps

## Trap 1: Stative Verbs in Continuous

```

```

## Trap 2: Since + Duration

```
âŒ She has lived here since 5 years.
âœ… She has lived here for 5 years.
âœ… She has lived here since 2018.
```

## Trap 3: Present Perfect with Past Time

```
âŒ I have visited Delhi yesterday.
âœ… I visited Delhi yesterday.
âœ… I have visited Delhi many times.
```

## Trap 4: By the time + Future

```
âŒ By the time he arrives, I will leave.
âœ… By the time he arrives, I will have left.

(The "leaving" must be completed before the "arriving")
```

## Trap 5: Wish + Past Tenses

```
PRESENT WISH (unreal now): Wish + Past Simple
"I wish I knew the answer." (but I don't)
"I wish I were taller." (but I'm not) [Use "were" for all]

PAST WISH (regret about past): Wish + Past Perfect
"I wish I had studied harder." (but I didn't)
```

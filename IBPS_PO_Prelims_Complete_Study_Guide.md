### Reading Comprehension - Master Strategies

#### **RC Strategy That Works:**
1. **First read the questions** (30 seconds) - Know what to look for
2. **Skim the passage** (90 seconds) - Get the main idea
3. **Answer questions** (3-4 minutes) - Use elimination technique

#### **Common RC Question Types & How to Tackle:**

**Type 1: Main Idea Questions**
- Look for: Topic sentence (usually first or last paragraph)
- Wrong options: Too specific or too broad
- **Example:** "The passage is primarily concerned with..."

**Type 2: Inference Questions** 
- Must be true based on passage, not general knowledge
- Look for clues like "suggest", "imply", "infer"
- **Trick:** Answer should be logical extension, not explicit statement

**Type 3: Tone/Attitude Questions**
- Author's emotional stance: Critical, appreciative, neutral, concerned
- Look for positive/negative words and overall sentiment

### Grammar & Error Detection - Score 100%

#### **Error Spotting - 5 Most Common Errors:**

**1. Subject-Verb Agreement**
- Wrong: "One of the boys are coming"
- Right: "One of the boys **is** coming"
- **Rule:** "One of/Each of/Either of" takes singular verb

**2. Tense Consistency** 
- Wrong: "He came yesterday and **tells** me the story"
- Right: "He came yesterday and **told** me the story"

**3. Preposition Errors**
- Wrong: "He is good **in** mathematics"
- Right: "He is good **at** mathematics"
- **Common:** Good at, Angry with, Different from, Concerned about

**4. Article Usage**
- Wrong: "He is **a** honest man"
- Right: "He is **an** honest man"
- **Rule:** Use 'an' before vowel sound (not vowel letter)

**5. Pronoun Reference**
- Wrong: "Each student must bring **their** book"
- Right: "Each student must bring **his/her** book"

#### **Sentence Improvement - Key Patterns:**

**Pattern 1: Parallelism**
- Wrong: "He likes singing, dancing, and **to paint**"
- Right: "He likes singing, dancing, and **painting**"

**Pattern 2: Comparative Degree**
- Wrong: "He is **more better** than his brother"
- Right: "He is **better** than his brother"

## üß† REASONING ABILITY - Master Every Type

### Syllogism - Score 100% (4-5 Questions)

#### **Venn Diagram Method - Never Fail:**

**Rule 1: Universal Statements**
- "All A are B" = Complete circle A inside circle B
- "No A are B" = Separate circles with no overlap
- "Some A are B" = Overlapping circles

**Rule 2: Conclusion Testing**
- All/No conclusions need definite relationship
- Some conclusions need possible relationship
- **Trick:** If you can draw counter-example, conclusion is false

**Solved Examples:**
```
Statements: All books are pages. Some pages are words.
Conclusions: 
I. All books are words
II. Some words are books

Solution:
I. FALSE - No direct relationship between books and words
II. POSSIBLE - Some area where pages overlap with words might contain books
Answer: Only II follows
```

#### **Coded Inequalities - 100% Accuracy (3-4 Questions)**

**Standard Code Pattern:**
```
A @ B means A > B
A # B means A < B  
A $ B means A ‚â• B
A % B means A ‚â§ B
A & B means A = B
```

**Solution Method:**
1. **Decode** all statements
2. **Combine** to form chain
3. **Check** each conclusion against the chain

**Example:**
```
P @ Q, Q # R, R $ S
Decoded: P > Q, Q < R, R ‚â• S
Chain: P > Q < R ‚â• S
Conclusion: P > S = FALSE (no direct comparison possible)
```

### Puzzles & Seating Arrangement - Master Strategy

#### **Linear Arrangement - Step-by-Step Method:**

**Step 1: Draw the Layout**
```
For 7 people in a row facing North:
Position: 1  2  3  4  5  6  7

```

**Step 2: Find Fixed Points**
- Look for definite positions (end positions, middle)
- Person sitting exactly at position X

**Step 3: Use Relative Information**  
- A sits 2nd to the right of B
- C sits between D and E

**Step 4: Fill Remaining Positions**

**Pro Tip:** Always start with most restrictive condition

#### **Circular Arrangement - Special Techniques:**

**Key Differences from Linear:**
- No end positions - everyone has 2 neighbors
- Facing center vs facing outside changes left/right
- Use one person as reference point

**Direction Rules:**
```
Facing Center: Left = Clockwise, Right = Anti-clockwise  
Facing Outside: Left = Anti-clockwise, Right = Clockwise
```

**Solved Example:**
8 people sit in circle facing center. A sits 3rd right of B.
**Solution:** If B is at position 1, A is at position 1+3 = 4

#### **Floor Puzzles - Master Formula:**

**Standard Pattern:** Different people on different floors
**Approach:**
1. **Fix reference point** (usually ground floor = 1)
2. **Use floor gap information** (2 floors above, 1 floor below)
3. **Apply elimination** for remaining positions

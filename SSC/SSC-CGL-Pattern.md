# SSC CGL Exam Pattern

## Overview

SSC CGL (Combined Graduate Level) examination is conducted in 4 tiers to recruit candidates for various Group B and Group C posts in ministries/departments/organizations of the Government of India.

## Tier-wise Exam Pattern

### Tier I (Preliminary Examination)

| Subject | Questions | Marks | Time | Negative Marking |
|---------|-----------|-------|------|------------------|
| General Intelligence and Reasoning | 25 | 50 | 60 minutes | 0.50 marks |
| General Knowledge and General Awareness | 25 | 50 | (Total time) | 0.50 marks |
| Quantitative Aptitude | 25 | 50 | 60 minutes | 0.50 marks |
| English Comprehension | 25 | 50 | (for all 4 sections) | 0.50 marks |
| **Total** | **100** | **200** | **60 minutes** | **0.50 marks per wrong answer** |

**Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
**Language:** Hindi and English (except English Comprehension)

### Tier II (Main Examination)

#### Paper I - Quantitative Abilities
- **Questions:** 100
- **Marks:** 200 (2 marks per question)
- **Time:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Negative Marking:** 0.50 marks per wrong answer
- **Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Compulsory for:** All candidates

#### Paper II - English Language and Comprehension
- **Questions:** 200
- **Marks:** 200 (1 mark per question)
- **Time:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Negative Marking:** 0.25 marks per wrong answer
- **Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Compulsory for:** All candidates

#### Paper III - Statistics
- **Questions:** 100
- **Marks:** 200 (2 marks per question)
- **Time:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Negative Marking:** 0.50 marks per wrong answer
- **Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Applicable for:** Statistical Investigator Grade II & Compiler posts only

#### Paper IV - General Studies (Finance and Economics)
- **Questions:** 100
- **Marks:** 200 (2 marks per question)
- **Time:** 2 hours (120 minutes)
- **Negative Marking:** 0.50 marks per wrong answer
- **Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
- **Applicable for:** Assistant Audit Officer & Assistant Accounts Officer posts only

### Tier III (Descriptive Examination)

| Component | Marks | Time |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Essay Writing | 60 | 60 minutes |
| Letter/Application Writing | 40 | (Total time) |
| **Total** | **100** | **60 minutes** |

**Mode:** Pen and Paper
**Language:** Hindi or English
**Nature:** Qualifying (33% marks required to qualify)

### Tier IV (Skill Test/Computer Proficiency Test)

#### Data Entry Speed Test (for DEO and Tax Assistant posts)
- **Speed Required:** 2000 key depressions per hour
- **Duration:** 15 minutes
- **Error Percentage:** Maximum 5% errors allowed
- **Nature:** Qualifying

#### Computer Proficiency Test (for Assistant Section Officer posts)
- **Components:** Word Processing, Spreadsheets, Generation of Slides
- **Duration:** 45 minutes total (15 minutes each component)
- **Nature:** Qualifying

## Qualification Criteria

### Tier I to Tier II
- Candidates are shortlisted based on normalized marks in Tier I
- Number of candidates shortlisted = 10 times the number of vacancies

### Tier II to Tier III
- Based on combined marks of applicable papers in Tier II
- Paper I + Paper II for most posts
- Paper I + Paper II + Paper III for Statistical posts
- Paper I + Paper II + Paper IV for Assistant Audit Officer/Assistant Accounts Officer posts

### Final Merit List
- Based on combined marks of Tier I + Tier II + Tier III
- Tier IV is qualifying only

## Important Notes

1. **Normalization:** Marks are normalized if exam is conducted in multiple shifts
2. **Calculator:** Not allowed in any tier
3. **Rough Sheet:** Provided during computer-based tests
4. **PWD Candidates:** Get additional time as per rules
5. **Scribe:** Allowed for visually impaired candidates
6. **Language:** All papers except English Comprehension available in Hindi and English

## Sectional Time Limits

### Tier I
- No sectional time limit
- Total 60 minutes for all 4 sections

### Tier II
- Each paper has individual time limit of 2 hours
- Papers conducted on different days/shifts

## Cut-off Trends

Cut-off marks vary each year based on:
- Number of vacancies
- Number of candidates
- Difficulty level of the exam
- Category of the candidate

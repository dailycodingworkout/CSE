# SSC MTS Exam Pattern

## Overview

SSC MTS (Multi Tasking Staff) examination is conducted to recruit Group 'C' non-gazetted, non-ministerial posts in various ministries/departments/organizations of the Government of India.

## Paper-wise Exam Pattern

### Paper I (Computer Based Test - Objective Type)

| Subject | Questions | Marks | Time | Negative Marking |
|---------|-----------|-------|------|------------------|
| General Intelligence and Reasoning | 25 | 25 | 90 minutes | 0.25 marks |
| Numerical Aptitude | 25 | 25 | (Total time) | 0.25 marks |
| General English | 25 | 25 | 90 minutes | 0.25 marks |
| General Awareness | 25 | 25 | (for all 4 sections) | 0.25 marks |
| **Total** | **100** | **100** | **90 minutes** | **0.25 marks per wrong answer** |

**Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
**Language:** Hindi and English (except General English section)
**Qualification:** Must score above cut-off marks to qualify for Paper II

### Paper II (Descriptive Paper)

| Component | Marks | Time | Mode |
|-----------|-------|------|------|
| Short Essay/Letter Writing | 50 | 30 minutes | Pen and Paper |
| **Total** | **50** | **30 minutes** | **Offline** |

**Language:** Hindi, English, or any of the 22 scheduled languages
**Nature:** Qualifying (minimum 33% marks i.e., 16.5 marks required)
**Word Limit:** Approximately 200-250 words

## Detailed Marking Scheme

### Paper I Scoring
- **Total Questions:** 100
- **Total Marks:** 100
- **Correct Answer:** +1 mark
- **Wrong Answer:** -0.25 marks
- **Unattempted:** 0 marks

### Section-wise Distribution in Paper I
| Subject | Questions | Marks | Weightage | Difficulty Level |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| General Intelligence and Reasoning | 25 | 25 | 25% | Moderate |
| Numerical Aptitude | 25 | 25 | 25% | Moderate |
| General English | 25 | 25 | 25% | Easy to Moderate |
| General Awareness | 25 | 25 | 25% | Moderate |

### Final Merit Calculation
**Final Merit = Paper I Marks Only**

*Note: Paper II is qualifying in nature and does not contribute to final merit ranking*

## Important Instructions

### General Guidelines
1. **Normalization:** Marks are normalized if exam conducted in multiple shifts
2. **Calculator:** Not allowed in any paper
3. **Rough Paper:** Provided during CBT
4. **ID Proof:** Valid photo ID mandatory
5. **Admit Card:** Must carry downloaded admit card

### Paper I Specific Instructions
- **No Sectional Time Limit**
- **Question Navigation:** Can move between questions freely
- **Language Selection:** Can view questions in Hindi or English (except English section)
- **Virtual Calculator:** Available for numerical calculations
- **Final Submission:** Must submit before time ends
- **Auto-submit:** System will auto-submit after 90 minutes

### Paper II Specific Instructions
- **Blue/Black Pen Only:** Use only blue or black ball point pen
- **OMR Sheet:** Proper marking required
- **Word Limit:** Stay within prescribed word limit
- **Legible Writing:** Ensure clear and readable handwriting
- **Language Consistency:** Maintain same language throughout
- **No Rough Work:** Write final answer only

## Time Management Strategy

### Paper I (90 minutes allocation)
- **General Intelligence & Reasoning:** 20-22 minutes
- **Numerical Aptitude:** 22-25 minutes
- **General English:** 18-22 minutes
- **General Awareness:** 15-18 minutes
- **Review and Corrections:** 8-10 minutes

### Section-wise Time per Question
| Section | Time per Question | Strategy |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| Reasoning | 45-60 seconds | Solve easy ones first |
| Numerical | 60-70 seconds | Skip lengthy calculations initially |
| English | 40-50 seconds | Focus on vocabulary and grammar |
| GK | 35-45 seconds | Attempt known ones quickly |

### Paper II (30 minutes)
- **Planning:** 3-5 minutes (outline key points)
- **Writing:** 22-25 minutes
- **Review:** 2-3 minutes (check grammar, spelling)

### Post Allocation
- **Home State Preference:** May get preference for home state/region

## Preparation Strategy

### 6-Month Preparation Plan

#### Months 1-2: Foundation Building
- **Study Plan:** 4-5 hours daily
- **Focus:** Basic concepts in all subjects
- **Resources:** Standard textbooks and basic study materials
- **Practice:** 20-30 questions daily from each section

#### Months 3-4: Intensive Practice
- **Study Plan:** 5-6 hours daily
- **Focus:** Advanced topics and problem-solving
- **Resources:** Previous year papers and mock tests
- **Practice:** Full-length mock tests weekly

#### Months 5-6: Revision and Mock Tests
- **Study Plan:** 6-7 hours daily
- **Focus:** Revision and test-taking strategies
- **Resources:** Revision notes and mock test series
- **Practice:** Daily mock tests and analysis

### Section-wise Preparation Strategy

#### General Intelligence & Reasoning
- **Strong Areas:** Focus on analogies, series, coding-decoding
- **Practice:** 25-30 questions daily
- **Time Target:** Complete section in 20 minutes
- **Tips:** Use elimination method for difficult questions

#### Numerical Aptitude
- **Foundation:** Strengthen basic arithmetic
- **Practice:** Focus on percentage, ratio, time-work problems
- **Speed:** Improve calculation speed
- **Tips:** Learn shortcut methods and tricks

#### General English
- **Vocabulary:** Learn 10-15 new words daily
- **Grammar:** Master basic grammar rules
- **Practice:** Read newspapers and magazines
- **Tips:** Focus on error detection and sentence improvement

#### General Awareness
- **Current Affairs:** Last 6 months events
- **Static GK:** Important facts and figures
- **Monthly Revision:** Current affairs magazines
- **Tips:** Make notes and revise regularly

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### During Preparation
1. **Ignoring Syllabus:** Not following prescribed syllabus
2. **Lack of Practice:** Insufficient practice of questions
3. **No Time Management:** Not practicing with time limits
4. **Overconfidence:** Neglecting easy topics
5. **No Revision:** Not revising covered topics

### During Exam
1. **Time Mismanagement:** Spending too much time on difficult questions
2. **Random Guessing:** Avoid due to negative marking
3. **Panic:** Stay calm and composed
4. **Technical Issues:** Report system problems immediately
5. **Incomplete Submission:** Ensure all answers are marked

## Mock Test Strategy

### Frequency
- **Initial Phase:** 2 mock tests per week
- **Intensive Phase:** 1 mock test daily
- **Final Phase:** 2 mock tests daily

### Analysis Process
1. **Score Analysis:** Track improvement in scores
2. **Time Analysis:** Monitor time spent per section
3. **Error Analysis:** Identify types of mistakes
4. **Topic-wise Analysis:** Find weak and strong areas
5. **Strategy Adjustment:** Modify approach based on analysis

## Final Preparation Tips

### Last Month
- **Focus:** Revision and practice tests only
- **New Topics:** Avoid learning new concepts
- **Current Affairs:** Update with latest events
- **Health:** Maintain good health and sleep schedule
- **Confidence:** Stay positive and confident

### Exam Day Preparation
- **Documents:** Carry admit card and valid ID
- **Reporting:** Reach center 30 minutes early
- **Relaxation:** Stay calm and composed
- **Strategy:** Follow planned exam strategy
- **Verification:** Check system functionality before starting

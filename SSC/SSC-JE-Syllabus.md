# SSC JE (Junior Engineer) Syllabus

## Examination Structure

The SSC JE examination is conducted in 2 papers:
- **Paper I**: Computer Based Examination (CBE) - Objective Type
- **Paper II**: Conventional (Pen & Paper) - Descriptive Type

## Eligibility

- **Educational Qualification**: Engineering Degree/Diploma in relevant discipline
- **Age Limit**: 18-32 years (relaxation as per government rules)

## Paper II Syllabus (Descriptive Paper)

**Duration:** 2 hours
**Maximum Marks:** 300 marks
**Mode:** Pen and Paper (Offline)
**Nature:** Qualifying (minimum 25% marks required)

### Part A - General Engineering (Civil & Structural) OR Electrical OR Mechanical (200 Marks)
- Same topics as Paper I Part C but in descriptive format
- Requires detailed solutions and explanations
- May include design problems, numerical calculations, and theoretical explanations

### Part B - Relevant Engineering Discipline (100 Marks)
- Advanced topics related to the specific engineering discipline
- Case studies and practical applications
- Design and analysis problems

## Important Notes

1. **Negative Marking:** 0.25 marks deducted for each wrong answer in Paper I
2. **Qualifying Paper:** Paper II is qualifying in nature (25% marks required)
3. **Final Merit:** Based on marks obtained in Paper I only
4. **Calculator:** Simple calculator allowed in Paper II
5. **Language:** Papers available in Hindi and English

## Preparation Strategy

1. **Understand the Syllabus:** Focus on core engineering subjects
2. **Practice Numericals:** Solve plenty of numerical problems
3. **Revision:** Regular revision of formulas and concepts
4. **Mock Tests:** Practice online mock tests regularly
5. **Previous Year Papers:** Analyze and solve previous year questions
6. **Time Management:** Practice completing tests within time limits

# General Instructions for SSC Examinations

## Common Guidelines for All SSC Examinations

### Application Process

1. **Online Application**: All applications must be submitted online through the official SSC website

3. **Payment Mode**: Online (Net Banking, Credit Card, Debit Card, UPI)
4. **Document Upload**: Scanned copies of required documents must be uploaded

### Required Documents

#### For Application
- Recent passport-size photograph (JPG format, 20KB-50KB)
- Scanned signature (JPG format, 10KB-20KB)
- Educational certificates
- Category certificate (if applicable)
- PWD certificate (if applicable)

#### For Document Verification (After Selection)
- Educational qualification certificates
- Date of birth certificate
- Caste/category certificate
- Experience certificate (where applicable)
- NOC from current employer (for employed candidates)

### Examination Guidelines

#### Before the Exam
- **Admit Card**: Download and print admit card from SSC website
- **Verification**: Verify all details on admit card
- **Reporting Time**: Reach exam center 30 minutes before exam time
- **Valid ID**: Carry valid photo ID proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Driving License, etc.)

#### During the Exam
- **Prohibited Items**:
  - Mobile phones
  - Electronic devices
  - Calculator (except where specifically allowed)
  - Books, notes, or written material
  - Watch (analog or digital)
- **Allowed Items**:
  - Admit card and valid ID
  - Blue/black ball point pen (for OMR/descriptive papers)
  - Rough sheets (provided at center)

#### Computer Based Tests (CBT)
- **Browser**: Special secure browser will be used
- **Practice**: Practice session before actual test
- **Navigation**: Can navigate between questions
- **Review**: Can review and change answers before final submission
- **Auto-submit**: Test will auto-submit after time completion

### Answer Marking Instructions

#### For OMR Sheets (if applicable)
- Use only blue/black ball point pen
- Completely darken the appropriate circle
- Do not make any stray marks
- Ensure answer sheet is not folded or damaged

#### For CBT
- Click on the appropriate option
- Use virtual calculator if provided
- Can change answers before final submission

### Negative Marking

Most SSC examinations have negative marking:
- **Tier I CBT**: 0.50 marks deduction for each wrong answer
- **Tier II CBT**: 0.25 to 0.50 marks deduction (varies by paper)
- **Descriptive Papers**: No negative marking

### Special Provisions

#### For PWD Candidates
- **Extra Time**: 20 minutes per hour of examination
- **Scribe**: Allowed for visually impaired candidates
- **Magnifying Glass**: Allowed for low vision candidates
- **Wheelchair**: Accessible examination centers

#### For Ex-Servicemen
- **Age Relaxation**: As per government rules
- **Fee Exemption**: Application fee exempted
- **Certificate**: Valid discharge certificate required

### Post-Exam Process

#### Result Declaration
- Results published on official SSC website
- Scorecard available for download
- Cut-off marks published with results

#### Document Verification
- **When**: After final result declaration
- **Where**: Regional SSC offices
- **Duration**: 3-7 days (as notified)
- **Documents**: Original certificates and photocopies

#### Final Merit List
- Based on combined score of all qualifying papers
- Published after document verification
- Final selection communicated via email/post

### Important Points to Remember

1. **Official Website**: Always refer to official SSC website (ssc.nic.in) for authentic information
2. **Notifications**: Read official notifications carefully
3. **Dates**: Keep track of important dates and deadlines

### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

**Q: Can I edit my application after submission?**
A: Limited corrections are allowed during specific correction windows as announced by SSC.

**Q: What if my name is misspelled in the admit card?**
A: Contact the concerned Regional Office immediately with supporting documents.

**Q: Can I appear for multiple SSC examinations simultaneously?**
A: Yes, you can appear for different SSC examinations if eligible and dates don't clash.

**Q: Is coaching necessary for SSC exams?**
A: Not mandatory. Self-study with proper books and practice can be sufficient.

**Q: How many attempts are allowed?**
A: No restriction on number of attempts (subject to age limit).

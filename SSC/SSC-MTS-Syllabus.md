# SSC MTS (Multi Tasking Staff) Syllabus

## Examination Structure

The SSC MTS examination is conducted in 2 papers:
- **Paper I**: Computer Based Examination (CBE) - Objective Type
- **Paper II**: Descriptive Paper in English/Hindi

## Eligibility

- **Educational Qualification**: Matriculation (10th Pass) from a recognized Board
- **Age Limit**: 18-25 years (relaxation as per government rules)

## Paper II Syllabus (Descriptive Paper)

**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Maximum Marks:** 50 marks
**Mode:** Pen and Paper mode
**Language:** Hindi, English, or any of the 22 scheduled languages of the Constitution

### Short Essay/Letter Writing (50 marks)

**Topics may include:**
- Social Issues
- Environmental Concerns
- Current Affairs
- General Topics of National Interest
- Government Policies
- Technology and Development
- Education and Health
- Rural Development
- Women Empowerment

**Types of Writing:**
- **Essay Writing** (Usually 200-250 words)
- **Letter Writing** (Formal/Informal Letters)
- **Application Writing** (Job applications, leave applications)
- **Report Writing** (Simple reports on given topics)

## Important Notes

1. **Negative Marking:** 0.25 marks deducted for each wrong answer in Paper I
2. **Qualifying Nature:** Paper II is qualifying in nature (minimum 33% marks required)
3. **Final Merit:** Based on marks obtained in Paper I only
4. **Normalization:** Applied if exam conducted in multiple shifts
5. **Language:** Paper I available in Hindi and English

## Posts Available

**Multi Tasking Staff (MTS) posts in various categories:**
- Group 'C' Non-Gazetted, Non-Ministerial posts
- Various Ministries/Departments/Organizations
- Support staff roles in government offices

**Typical Job Responsibilities:**
- Office maintenance work
- Cleaning and upkeep of office premises
- Basic clerical support
- Assistance in office operations
- Security-related duties (where assigned)

## Preparation Strategy

### Paper I Preparation (90 minutes)
- **General Intelligence & Reasoning:** 20-22 minutes
- **Numerical Aptitude:** 22-25 minutes
- **General English:** 20-22 minutes
- **General Awareness:** 18-20 minutes
- **Review:** 3-5 minutes

### Subject-wise Preparation Tips

#### General Intelligence & Reasoning
- **Daily Practice:** 20-25 questions daily
- **Pattern Recognition:** Focus on series and analogies
- **Speed Building:** Practice with time constraints
- **Weak Areas:** Identify and work on weak topics

#### Numerical Aptitude
- **Basic Mathematics:** Strengthen fundamentals
- **Formula Practice:** Regular revision of formulas
- **Speed Calculation:** Practice mental math
- **Previous Papers:** Solve last 5-10 years' questions

#### General English
- **Vocabulary Building:** Learn 10 new words daily
- **Grammar Rules:** Master basic grammar concepts
- **Reading Habit:** Read newspapers and magazines
- **Practice Tests:** Regular practice of English questions

#### General Awareness
- **Current Affairs:** Last 6-12 months current events
- **Static GK:** Focus on important facts and figures
- **Monthly Magazines:** Read monthly current affairs magazines
- **Revision:** Regular revision of covered topics

### Paper II Preparation
1. **Language Skills:** Improve writing skills in chosen language
2. **Essay Structure:** Learn proper essay writing format
3. **Current Topics:** Stay updated with current social issues
4. **Practice Writing:** Write essays and letters regularly
5. **Time Management:** Complete within 30 minutes

# SSC JE (Junior Engineer) Exam Pattern

## Overview

SSC JE (Junior Engineer) examination is conducted to recruit Junior Engineers in Civil, Electrical, and Mechanical disciplines for various Central Government ministries/departments and organizations.

## Paper-wise Exam Pattern

### Paper I (Computer Based Test - Objective Type)

| Subject | Questions | Marks | Time | Negative Marking |
|---------|-----------|-------|------|------------------|
| Part A: General Intelligence & Reasoning | 50 | 50 | 2 hours | 0.25 marks |
| Part B: General Awareness | 50 | 50 | (Total time) | 0.25 marks |
| Part C: General Engineering | 100 | 100 | 2 hours | 0.25 marks |
| **Total** | **200** | **200** | **2 hours** | **0.25 marks per wrong answer** |

**Mode:** Computer Based Test (CBT)
**Language:** Hindi and English
**Nature:** Screening Test

#### Part C - General Engineering Options:
- **Civil & Structural Engineering** (for Civil JE posts)
- **Electrical Engineering** (for Electrical JE posts)  
- **Mechanical Engineering** (for Mechanical JE posts)

### Paper II (Descriptive - Conventional Type)

| Subject | Marks | Time | Mode |
|---------|-------|------|------|
| Part A: General Engineering | 200 | 2 hours | Pen and Paper |
| Part B: Relevant Engineering Discipline | 100 | (Total time) | Pen and Paper |
| **Total** | **300** | **2 hours** | **Offline** |

**Language:** Hindi or English
**Nature:** Qualifying (minimum 25% marks required to qualify)
**Calculator:** Simple calculator allowed

## Detailed Marking Scheme

### Paper I Scoring
- **Total Questions:** 200
- **Total Marks:** 200
- **Correct Answer:** +1 mark
- **Wrong Answer:** -0.25 marks
- **Unattempted:** 0 marks

### Section-wise Distribution in Paper I
| Section | Questions | Marks | Weightage | Difficulty Level |
|---------|-----------|-------|-----------|------------------|
| General Intelligence & Reasoning | 50 | 50 | 25% | Moderate |
| General Awareness | 50 | 50 | 25% | Moderate |
| General Engineering | 100 | 100 | 50% | Moderate to High |

### Paper II Scoring
- **No Negative Marking**
- **Descriptive Answers Required**
- **Qualifying Nature:** 25% marks required to qualify
- **Final Merit:** Based on Paper I marks only

## Important Instructions

### General Guidelines
1. **Normalization:** Marks normalized if exam conducted in multiple shifts
2. **OMR Sheet:** Paper II requires proper OMR marking
3. **Calculator:** Simple calculator allowed in Paper II only
4. **Rough Work:** Use only provided rough sheets
5. **ID Proof:** Valid photo ID mandatory

### Paper I Specific Instructions
- **No Sectional Time Limit**
- **Question Navigation:** Can move between questions freely
- **Language Selection:** Can view questions in Hindi or English
- **Final Submission:** Must submit before time ends
- **Technical Issues:** Report immediately to invigilator

### Paper II Specific Instructions
- **Blue/Black Pen Only:** Use only blue or black ball point pen
- **Clear Handwriting:** Ensure legible handwriting
- **Proper Diagrams:** Draw neat and labeled diagrams where required
- **Formula Usage:** Can write relevant formulas
- **Time Management:** Allocate time properly between sections
- **Answer Completeness:** Provide complete solutions with steps

## Posts and Departments

### Available Posts by Engineering Discipline

#### Civil Engineering Posts
- Junior Engineer (Civil) - CPWD, Railways, PWD
- Assistant Engineer (Civil) - Various departments
- Sub Divisional Engineer - State PWDs (deputation)

#### Electrical Engineering Posts  
- Junior Engineer (Electrical) - Railways, CPWD, Power Grid
- Assistant Engineer (Electrical) - Power sector organizations
- Electrical Inspector - Various departments

#### Mechanical Engineering Posts
- Junior Engineer (Mechanical) - Railways, Defence, PSUs
- Assistant Engineer (Mechanical) - Various technical departments
- Boiler Inspector - Factory inspectorates

### Salary and Benefits
- **Pay Scale:** Level 6 (₹35,400 - ₹1,12,400)
- **In-hand Salary:** ₹35,000 - ₹45,000 (approximately)
- **Grade Pay:** As per 7th Pay Commission
- **Allowances:** DA, HRA, TA as per government rules
- **Other Benefits:** Medical facilities, LTC, pension, professional development

## Preparation Strategy

### Paper I Time Management (2 hours = 120 minutes)
- **General Intelligence & Reasoning:** 25-30 minutes
- **General Awareness:** 20-25 minutes
- **General Engineering:** 50-60 minutes
- **Review and Final Check:** 10-15 minutes

### Paper II Strategy
- **Part A (General Engineering):** 80-90 minutes
- **Part B (Specific Discipline):** 30-40 minutes
- **Review:** 10 minutes

### Subject-wise Preparation Tips

#### General Intelligence & Reasoning
- **Focus Areas:** Analogies, coding-decoding, series completion
- **Practice:** Daily practice of 25-30 questions
- **Time per Question:** 30-40 seconds average

#### General Awareness
- **Current Affairs:** Last 12 months current events
- **Static GK:** Focus on awards, books, authors, important days
- **Technical GK:** Basic engineering awareness

#### General Engineering
- **Core Subjects:** Focus on fundamental concepts
- **Formula Practice:** Regular revision of important formulas
- **Previous Papers:** Solve last 10 years' questions
- **Conceptual Clarity:** Understand concepts rather than cramming

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Paper I Mistakes
1. **Over-thinking:** Don't spend too much time on single question
2. **Calculation Errors:** Be careful with numerical calculations
3. **Random Guessing:** Avoid due to negative marking
4. **Time Pressure:** Don't panic in last 15 minutes
5. **Technical Difficulties:** Report system issues immediately

### Paper II Mistakes
1. **Poor Handwriting:** Ensure readable handwriting
2. **Incomplete Answers:** Provide complete solutions
3. **Wrong Approach:** Use correct problem-solving methods  
4. **Diagram Mistakes:** Draw accurate and neat diagrams
5. **Time Mismanagement:** Don't spend too much time on single question
